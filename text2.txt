1. Heath's Theorem (The Foundation of 3NF/BCNF)Heath's Theorem relies on Functional Dependencies (FDs). A functional dependency ($X \rightarrow Y$) means that if you know the value of $X$, you can uniquely determine the value of $Y$.The Theorem:If a relation $R$ with attributes $(A, B, C)$ satisfies the functional dependency $A \rightarrow B$, then $R$ can be losslessly decomposed into two smaller relations:$R_1(A, B)$$R_2(A, C)$What this means in plain English:If column $A$ uniquely identifies column $B$, you can safely peel column $B$ off into its own table alongside $A$. You leave $A$ and everything else ($C$) in the other table. When you join them back using $A$, you will get your exact original data back.Example of Heath's TheoremImagine a table tracking which employees are working on which projects.Original Relation: $R(EmpID, EmpName, Project)$Functional Dependency: $EmpID \rightarrow EmpName$ (An ID uniquely determines the person's name).
